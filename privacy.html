Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 17, 2026

SolarVelocity AI ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of our clients and their customers. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and share personal information in the course of providing our AI-driven sales automation and lead qualification services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Client Information

We collect information provided by our business clients, including:

  • Name, business address, and contact details.
  • Payment information (processed securely through Stripe).
  • CRM access credentials and integration data.

1.2 Lead and Customer Data

On behalf of our clients, we process information about their leads and customers, including:

  • Name, phone number, and email address.
  • Residential address and utility bill information (kWh usage, monthly cost).
  • Property ownership status.
  • Call recordings and transcripts from interactions with our AI Sales Concierge.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information collected to:

  • Provide and maintain our AI Sales Concierge (Solar Sam) services.
  • Qualify leads based on client-specific criteria (e.g., homeowner status, bill amount).
  • Book appointments directly into client CRMs and calendars.
  • Improve our AI models and service quality through anonymized data analysis.
  • Communicate with clients regarding service updates and support.
  • Process payments and prevent fraudulent transactions.

3. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal information. We share data only with:

  • Sub-processors: Third-party providers necessary for service delivery, including AI infrastructure (e.g., Vapi, ElevenLabs, OpenAI) and communication platforms (e.g., Twilio, Make.com).
  • Client Direction: We transfer qualified lead data and appointment details to the client's designated CRM.
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or to protect our rights and safety.

4. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect data from unauthorized access, disclosure, or alteration. This includes encryption of data in transit and at rest, and strict access controls for our internal systems.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by legal and contractual obligations. Clients may request the deletion of their data upon termination of service.

6. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal information under laws such as the CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) or GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation). These rights may include:

  • The right to access and receive a copy of your data.
  • The right to request the correction or deletion of your data.
  • The right to opt-out of certain data processing activities.

7. International Data Transfers

Data may be processed in the United States and other countries where our sub-processors operate. We ensure that such transfers comply with applicable data protection laws.

8. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify clients of any material changes via email or a prominent notice on our website.
